Recently Extinct Animals


Some animals that have gone extinct in recent years:

  1. Pinta Island Tortoise

    2. Date of extinction: 2012

    Cause of extinction: mainly by hunting

    Most had been wiped out by the 19th century due to hunting. Last single male found in 1971, named Lonesome George, died on 24 June 2012.


  2. Javan Tiger

    3. Date of extinction: 2003

    Cause of extinction: hunting, lack of prey, loss of forest habitat

    They used to inhabit their native land Java, Indoneisa. The native people regarded them as pests in the mid 1800s - mid 1900s however, and chased them to remote mountainous areas. The remaining tigers were moved to the Meru Betiri National Park Reserve by 1972, but the number of tigers still diminished. The last few were seen 1976 before officially declared extinct in 2003.


  3. Western Black Rhinoceros

    4. Date of extinction: 2011

    Cause of extinction: loss of habitat, poaching

    A subspecies of black rhinos. They were hunted for their horns that were sold on the black market, which were beautiful and believed to have medicinal properties. Last reported sighting in 2006 in Cameroon's Northern Province. Declared offically extinct in 2011 by the IUCN.


  4. The Spix's Macaw

    5. Date of extinction: around 2000 (Extinct in the wild)

    Cause of extinction: loss of habitat, illegal pet trade

    Native to Brazil. Last-known wild Spix's Macaw died in 2000, but it was estimated by BirdLife International that about 60 - 80 birds are held in captivity. Also known as the Blue Macaw, they are now classified as extinct in the wild by IUCN and BirdLife International.
